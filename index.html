Film Awards
Born 1972 in Belgrade, Yugoslavia.

Director, screenwriter and composer.

Started as a frontman of the rock band BAAL in 1990.
After the band split in 1995, continued as a film composer.
That same year he won his first award for best original film music
(Golden Mimosa at the Herceg-Novi Yugoslav Film Festival).

The youngest artist in Yugoslavia winning the Gold Award for the
best original film music three times (Composer of the year in 2002)

In 1997 enroled to the Art Academy in Belgrade, film and televison
directing department. That same year worked in Paris on album
One Trip One Noise with french band Noir Desir.
The album was published in 1998 by Barclay/Polygram.

From 1995 to 2002 directed seven short films and
three theatre performances.

Graduated in 2002 at the Art Academy in Belgrade with
his short fiction film THE MARGIN.

In 2003 THE MARGIN won The Gold Special Jury Award
at the 36th Worldfest Film Festival in Houston, USA.

From 2000 to 2007 won nine Yugoslav and international
awards for film directing.

From 2000 to 2008 worked on numerous television productions
in Serbia as director ( 85 episodes of JELENA TV series in 2004).
For JELENA nominated in 2005 for the best director at Discop, Budapest, Hungary.
